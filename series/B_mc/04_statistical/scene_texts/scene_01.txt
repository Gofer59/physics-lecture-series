You ran your MCMC chain. Averaged your observable. Divided sigma by the square root of N. The number you report is wrong. By a factor of the square root of two tau-int, which can be ten, or fifty, or a thousand.

MCMC samples are correlated, and the naive independent-sample error formula silently treats them as independent. Nearly every quantitative MC paper lives or dies by how honestly it handles this.

If your error bar looks too good, it is too good. Fix that.
