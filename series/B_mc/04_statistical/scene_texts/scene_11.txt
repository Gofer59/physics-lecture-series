Your error bar is itself an estimate, with its own error. Relative uncertainty on sigma scales as the square root of twice the window plus one over N, roughly the square root of four tau-int over N.

Rule of thumb. N at least one hundred times tau-int for a ten-percent error on the error. A two-and-a-half-sigma discrepancy means nothing if sigma itself is off by a factor of two. Always quote tau-int alongside your main number.
