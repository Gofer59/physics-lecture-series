To compute tau-int, you estimate the autocorrelation function and sum it. But at large lag, estimates are dominated by noise. You must truncate.

Common rules. Cut when the autocorrelation estimate turns negative. Or at a window roughly six times the emerging tau-int, self-consistently. Or fit an exponential at intermediate lags. Either way, truncation is a bias-variance trade, and you don't get tau-int for free.
