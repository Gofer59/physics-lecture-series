The easiest kind of parallelism. Run P independent replicas with different random seeds. Average at the end. Error drops by the square root of P. Zero inter-processor communication. Perfect scaling.

Great for error estimation via many short runs. For averaging over quenched disorder. And as the communication substrate of parallel tempering. When it works, nothing beats it.

The first rule of parallel Monte Carlo. If you can run independent copies, do that. Then have lunch.
