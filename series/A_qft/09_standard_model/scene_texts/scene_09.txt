Quarks and gluons never appear alone. Produced in a collision, they immediately hadronize into collimated sprays of particles called jets.

A three-jet event seen in 1979 was the direct discovery of the gluon. Modern jet algorithms, like anti-k-T, cluster particles into well-defined objects whose properties extract the strong coupling with percent-level precision.

Quarks are shy. Jets are what you get when you point a detector at their general direction.
