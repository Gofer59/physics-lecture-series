If you tried to quantize the Dirac field with ordinary commutators, like we did for Klein-Gordon, the Hamiltonian would be unbounded below. You could keep making the vacuum more negative forever.

The only fix is to impose anticommutation relations. The creation and annihilation operators anticommute instead of commute. Two consequences follow immediately. Acting twice with the same creation operator gives zero — Pauli exclusion. And all particles with half-integer spin are automatically fermions. This is the spin-statistics theorem.

Fermions fundamentally do not want to share. This is why electrons stack into shells, and your chair holds you up.
