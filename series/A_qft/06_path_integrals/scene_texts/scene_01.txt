Canonical quantization turns fields into operators. It insists on a preferred direction of time. Feynman found this unsatisfying, so he proposed something radically different.

Compute quantum amplitudes by summing over every conceivable history. Weight each one by the exponential of i times the classical action. That sum is the path integral. In quantum field theory, the histories are not particle trajectories. They are entire field configurations spread across all of spacetime.

Feynman's idea was: instead of asking what happens, just average over everything that could happen, with a funny oscillatory weight. And somehow it works.
