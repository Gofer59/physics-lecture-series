Path integrals won the modern quantum field theory textbook because they do the things canonical quantization does poorly.

Gauge theories are natural. Symmetries are manifest. Non-perturbative effects are tractable. Statistical mechanics and lattice simulation are a Wick rotation away. The formalism even extends cleanly to gravity, and has generated every serious attempt at quantum gravity since the 1960s.

Canonical quantization still has its place. But the path integral is the lingua franca.
