Apply the same trick with a symmetry transformation instead of a shift. If the path-integral measure is invariant under, say, a global U(1) rotation, an identity drops out: the Ward-Takahashi identity.

It relates the divergence of the current to a sum of contact terms. In QED this is the same Ward identity we saw from canonical reasoning, now derived in two lines from symmetry of the measure.

Every continuous symmetry generates an analogous identity. These are what tie the whole theory together.
