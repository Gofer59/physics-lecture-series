For a free scalar theory, the action is quadratic in the field, so the path integral is Gaussian. Complete the square, do the integral, and a clean answer appears.

Z of J equals Z of zero times the exponential of minus one-half J times the Feynman propagator times J.

This is exactly the same Feynman propagator we built from canonical quantization. The two methods agree. All the hard work of Dyson, Wick, and normal ordering is reproduced by a single Gaussian integral.
