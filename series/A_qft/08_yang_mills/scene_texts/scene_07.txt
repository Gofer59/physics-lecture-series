Quantizing a gauge theory means fixing the gauge. That introduces a determinant that cannot be absorbed away.

Faddeev and Popov showed the determinant is equivalent to adding new anticommuting scalar fields. They are called ghosts. Ghosts never appear as external particles.

Their only job is to appear inside loops, with the right sign, and cancel the unphysical polarizations of the gauge bosons. That cancellation is what preserves unitarity.
