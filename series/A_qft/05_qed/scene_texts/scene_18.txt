QED is the prototype of a gauge theory. Replace its U(1) symmetry by SU(3) and you get QCD. Replace it by SU(2) times U(1) and you get the electroweak theory.

Replace its single vector boson by eight gluons, or three weak bosons, and the rest of the machinery survives. The Feynman rules, the Ward-like identities, the principle that propagators and vertices define the theory — all of it carries through. QED is the gateway to the Standard Model.
