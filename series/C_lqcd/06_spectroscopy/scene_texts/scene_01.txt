You spent millions of core-hours generating gauge configurations. What do you do with them?

The flagship output of lattice QCD is the hadron spectrum. Masses of pions, kaons, protons, neutrons, Delta baryons, glueballs — all computed from first principles, with no free parameters beyond the quark masses and an overall scale.

In 2008 the BMW collaboration published the light hadron spectrum agreeing with experiment to within a few percent.

The spectrum of hadrons, from first principles, to percent accuracy. This is what QCD looks like when it works.
