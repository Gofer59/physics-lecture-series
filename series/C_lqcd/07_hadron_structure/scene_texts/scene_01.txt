Two-point functions give hadron masses. For precision flavor physics — CKM elements, B-meson mixing, meson decays — we need matrix elements of operators between hadron states. That requires three-point functions.

For internal structure — how a proton's momentum and spin are shared between quarks and gluons — we need parton distributions.

Lattice QCD goes beyond the spectrum when it starts answering the real question: what is a proton made of?

Masses were the easy part. Now we ask the proton to open up and talk about its feelings.
