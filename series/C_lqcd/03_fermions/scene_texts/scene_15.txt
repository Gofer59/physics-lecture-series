To compute any hadron correlator, you need the quark propagator — the inverse of the Dirac operator in a given gauge background. D is a sparse matrix of dimension twelve times the lattice volume.

You do not invert it explicitly. You use iterative Krylov solvers — conjugate gradient, BiCGStab, GMRES. Cost scales like one over the quark mass squared near the chiral limit. Solvers are where most of the wall-clock time goes.
