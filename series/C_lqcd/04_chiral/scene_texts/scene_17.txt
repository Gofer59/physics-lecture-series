Ginsparg–Wilson fermions — overlap or domain wall — cost ten to a hundred times Wilson per Dirac inversion. That cost buys you exact chiral symmetry, integer topological charge, direct access to the condensate, and controllable chiral extrapolations.

For most precision physics, this is worth it. For others, improved Wilson or staggered at cheap cost is good enough. Every collaboration picks a spot on the trade-off curve. Chiral symmetry: the fermion feature that costs roughly as much as a new supercomputer.
