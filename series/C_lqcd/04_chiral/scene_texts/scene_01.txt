Massless quarks come in two independent chiralities: left and right. The QCD Lagrangian in the massless limit treats them as two decoupled worlds, related only by color. This is chiral symmetry.

But the physical world has pions that are absurdly light compared to other hadrons. Baryons whose masses refuse to line up in mirror multiplets. A nucleon built almost entirely from binding energy. All of that is chiral symmetry — spontaneously broken by the vacuum. Pions are light because they are the vacuum remembering a broken symmetry. Physics: where a ghost determines the mass spectrum.
