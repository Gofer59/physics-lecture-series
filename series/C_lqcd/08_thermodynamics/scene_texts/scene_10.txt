Couple the Higgs to a heavy fermion — a Yukawa interaction. The Higgs quartic coupling runs under the renormalization group, and at high scales it can drive the effective potential unstable, threatening the vacuum.

Lattice simulations of Yukawa models probe this non-perturbatively and give lower bounds on the Higgs mass.

With the top quark mass close to its measured value and the Higgs at one hundred twenty-five GeV, the vacuum is metastable — stable on cosmological timescales, but not forever.

Our vacuum has a shelf life. It is very long, but it is not infinite. Lattice QCD gave us a receipt.
