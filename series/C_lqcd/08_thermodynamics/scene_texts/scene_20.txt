Three series. Twenty-three videos. One argument: quantum field theory is not just a framework of equations. It is a functioning computational science.

You have seen the Lagrangian give rise to the path integral. The path integral Wick-rotated to statistical mechanics. Statistical mechanics sampled by Monte Carlo. Monte Carlo applied to a non-Abelian gauge theory on a lattice. And that lattice producing the proton, the pion, the equation of state of the early universe, and bounds on the Higgs.

What began as blackboard physics is now exascale computational physics. That is where the story ends, and where the next one begins.

The physicists of 1974 had a theory they could not solve. The physicists of today have one they can. Slowly, expensively, at sub-percent precision. It turns out that is enough.
